# PIA Research Roadmap (Accuracy First)

**Version:** 1.0
**Status:** Research Phase
**Objective:** Transform PIA into the most mathematically rigorous and accurate organizational intelligence engine.

---

# Vision

The first development phase of PIA focused on **building a complete intelligence engine**.

The next phase focuses exclusively on **maximizing inference accuracy**.

No major product features, dashboards, databases, or UI will be prioritized until the mathematical foundations of every intelligence layer have been thoroughly researched, validated, and benchmarked.

The guiding principle is:

> **Every layer should infer organizational intelligence with the highest possible accuracy, explainability, and generalization capability.**

---

# Research Philosophy

Instead of adding more features, every layer will undergo an iterative research cycle.

For each layer:

1. Understand the current implementation.
2. Identify mathematical assumptions.
3. Identify weaknesses.
4. Study state-of-the-art research.
5. Design improved mathematical models.
6. Implement alternatives.
7. Benchmark against the previous version.
8. Measure accuracy.
9. Retain only models that improve measurable performance.
10. Document all findings.

No layer is considered complete until its mathematical model has been validated.

# Research Rules

During this phase:

* No UI development.
* No dashboard development.
* No database work unless required for experiments.
* No feature additions unless they directly improve inference accuracy.
* Every new mathematical model must outperform the previous one before adoption.
* Every improvement must be documented with equations, assumptions, experiments, and benchmark results.

---

# Long-Term Goal

PIA should evolve from a heuristic organizational analytics system into a scientifically validated organizational intelligence framework capable of accurately inferring expertise, ownership, risk, resilience, and organizational health across diverse software engineering environments.

The success of this phase will not be measured by the number of features added, but by the measurable improvement in inference accuracy, mathematical rigor, explainability, and reproducibility.
