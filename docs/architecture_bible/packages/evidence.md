# Evidence Package (The Information Organizer)

This package contains the logic for **Evidence**. When raw data (Events) comes in from GitHub or Jira, it is messy and disconnected. The Evidence engine translates this chaos into a highly structured format (The Knowledge Graph) that the Cognitive Brain can actually understand and reason about.

Because this package manages the core memory and intelligence graph of the entire project, it is split into specialized sub-departments. Here is exactly how data flows through them.

---

## 1. `synthesis/` (The Assembler)
**Purpose**: To turn dumb facts into smart interpretations.
**Detailed Workflow**:
- A raw "Event" might say: *"Bob pushed a commit containing 4,000 lines of code to auth.py."*
- The `synthesis` engine looks at this and creates multiple "Evidence" cards. 
- It creates Evidence A: *"Bob is now the primary expert in auth.py."*
- It creates Evidence B: *"auth.py has just become highly complex and prone to bugs."*
- **Why we need it**: The AI Brain is too busy to read raw commits. The synthesis layer does the heavy lifting of reading the code and extracting the hidden meaning before the AI ever looks at it.

## 2. `semantic/` & `ontology/` (The Dictionary of Reality)
**Purpose**: Ensures the system actually understands what words mean, preventing the AI from getting confused.
**Detailed Workflow**:
- AI models often struggle with synonyms. If a Jira ticket says "Fixed a defect," and a GitHub commit says "Resolved a bug," the AI might think those are two different things.
- The `ontology/` script forces a strict vocabulary. It intercepts the words "defect", "issue", and "glitch" and maps them all strictly to the internal concept `BUG_FIX`.
- The `semantic/` script ensures that every single piece of Evidence generated by the Assembler strictly follows this dictionary.

## 3. `graph/` & `knowledge/` (The Spiderweb)
**Purpose**: To take the synthesized Evidence and weave it into a massive, searchable web of connections.
**Detailed Workflow**:
- Once Evidence is generated, it cannot just sit in a list. It must be connected to everything else.
- The `graph/` scripts take the new Evidence ("Bob is the expert in auth.py") and draws a physical line (an edge) between the Node representing Bob and the Node representing the file `auth.py`.
- It also looks for existing lines. If there is already a line saying "Alice is the expert in auth.py", the `knowledge/` script updates the graph to show that Alice and Bob now share ownership of the file.

## 4. `correlation/` (The Detective's Assistant)
**Purpose**: To automatically find hidden relationships without a human asking for them.
**Detailed Workflow**:
- This script constantly scans the Spiderweb looking for patterns. 
- If it notices that every time Developer A edits the frontend, Developer B edits the backend exactly one hour later, it automatically creates a new piece of Evidence: *"Strong correlation between Developer A and Developer B's workflows."*
- **Why we need it**: This allows the AI to recommend Bob as a reviewer for Alice's code, even if Bob and Alice have never explicitly spoken to each other.

## 5. `ranking/` (The Sorter)
**Purpose**: To prevent the AI from being overwhelmed by useless information.
**Detailed Workflow**:
- If you ask the AI "What is Alice working on?", there might be 10,000 pieces of evidence connected to Alice. The AI cannot read 10,000 things at once.
- The `ranking/` scripts sort the evidence by importance. Fixing a typo gets a rank of 1. Architecting a new database gets a rank of 100. The AI is only fed the top 50 highest-ranked pieces of evidence, ensuring it focuses on what matters.

## 6. `confidence/` & `validation/` (The Lie Detector)
**Purpose**: To ensure the Knowledge Graph is never poisoned by bad data.
**Detailed Workflow**:
- Before any Evidence is permanently written to the Graph, it goes through a lie detector.
- If the system sees Evidence claiming that a Junior developer who joined yesterday is the primary expert of the 5-year-old codebase, the `confidence/` script flags it as highly improbable.
- The `validation/` script will then reject the Evidence or lower its confidence score to 1%, ensuring the AI does not trust it during future decision-making.

## 7. `lifecycle/` (The Archivist)
**Purpose**: To simulate the fading of human memory over time.
**Detailed Workflow**:
- Evidence cannot last forever at 100% strength. If someone wrote a file 3 years ago and hasn't touched it since, their expertise is fading.
- The `lifecycle/` script runs every night, looking at old Evidence. It applies a mathematical "decay curve", slowly degrading the confidence score of old information until it is eventually archived into deep storage.

## 8. `query/` & `api/` (The Search Engine)
**Purpose**: The actual interface for pulling data out of the Spiderweb.
**Detailed Workflow**:
- Defines the incredibly fast database queries required to traverse millions of nodes in milliseconds. It allows the Cognitive Engine to ask complex questions like: *"Find me all developers who have touched a Python file in the last 6 months, who are also correlated with Alice."*

## 9. `streaming/` & `plugins/` (The Highway and Add-ons)
**Purpose**: Scalability and Extensibility.
**Detailed Workflow**:
- `streaming/` ensures that if a massive project with 100,000 commits is imported all at once, the system doesn't crash. It queues the events on a multi-lane highway, processing them at a safe speed.
- `plugins/` allows external companies to write their own custom Evidence translators. If a company wants to ingest data from a proprietary tool (like an internal HR system), they can drop a plugin here without rewriting the core engine.
